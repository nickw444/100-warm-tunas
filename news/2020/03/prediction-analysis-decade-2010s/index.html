Prediction Analysis Decade 2010s

Mar 22, 2020 • Nick Whyte

Throughout the last 6 weeks, 100 Warm Tunas has been aggregating your votes posted to popular social networks to provide the internet’s most accurate prediction of triple j’s Hottest 100 of the Decade.

Summary

  • We collected 3,181 entries.
  • We tallied 29,123 votes across these entries.
  • triple j counted approximately 1,800,000 votes.
  • Therefore, we collected a sample of 1.62%.
  • 11.2% of votes were collected via Instagram direct message.
  • We did not successfully predict #1
  • We predicted 6 out of the top 10 songs.
  • We predicted 25 out of the top 30 songs.
  • We predicted 79 out of the 100 songs played in the countdown.

Evidently the amount of data collected was less than the Hottest 100 of 2019 however, our prediction still performed better when looking at the overall count of predicted songs in the top 100.

Technical Analysis

Similar to the Hottest 100 of 2019, we were not able to predict the outright winner of the countdown ,however we did successfully predict 25 of the songs to rank in the top 30.

The accuracy of the top 10 is at 70%, the top 30 at 90%, and the top 100 at 80%. Whilst the accuracy across the top 200 stays almost constant these are some clear improvements over the results when Instagram feed images are included.

This trend is really interesting because counting just these sources means only 12,247 votes across 1,352 entries are counted toward the tally (compared to 29,123 across 3,181 entries). Perhaps this bias is an indication that it might be time to pull the plug on collecting data from the Instagram Feed and rely on these newer, more accurate sources?

It comes as no surprise that the most popular day for people to share their votes was on the day of voting opened with 442 entries collected, followed by the day voting closed with 391 entries collected.

Wrap Up

Whilst we didn’t pick the outright winner, we did successfully predict 25 of the 30 of the songs which landed in the top 30, as well as 79 of the top 100. As mentioned in the 2019 countdown wrap-up, with the shrinking amount of data and general biases across different voting sources, it’s getting harder and harder to pick the outright winner with certainty.

We’d like to thank everyone who shared their votes, visited our site or interacted with us on Instagram. Without our audience, 100 Warm Tunas wouldn’t be possible.

We are looking forward to running the prediction again in December for triple j’s Hottest 100 of 2020. Time to start collating that shortlist now!
