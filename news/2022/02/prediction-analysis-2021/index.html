Prediction Analysis 2021

Feb 1, 2022 • Nick Whyte

Wow! What a killer line up the Hottest 100 of 2021 was! Triple J really mixed things up this year by playing the Hottest 200 the day before the Hottest 100. This gave us an upper hand to improve our predictions, by allowing us to eliminate songs from the Hottest 100 which had ranked in the Hottest 200 on the previous day. In doing so, we managed to take our top 100 prediction accuracy from 73% to 82% of the top 100 songs.

As per usual, this year we continued to collect data across the following five sources:

  • Instagram Posts
  • Instagram DMs
  • Instagram Stories
  • Twitter Posts
  • Reddit Comments

Accuracy of Predicted Ranks

Making exact predictions is difficult, especially when you only have a small fraction of the data. The data will generally give you a good indication, but will never allow you to predict, with certainty, that a particular outcome will occur. This is the reason we implemented confidence intervals on our predictions for the top 10.
