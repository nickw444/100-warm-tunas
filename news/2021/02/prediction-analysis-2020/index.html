Prediction Analysis 2020

Feb 11, 2021 • Nick Whyte

100 Warm Tunas of 2020 was crazy! We saw our predictions rapidly change throughout the voting period, with Spacey Jane’s Booster Seat predicted for first when we first published, to be trumped by Glass Animal’s Heat Waves on the final day of data collection.

This year we introduced a new feature on our website to allow users to select the social mediums to be included in the prediction, allowing users to fine-tune the prediction that you see.

This year we continued to collect data across the following five sources:

  • Instagram Posts
  • Instagram DMs
  • Instagram Stories
  • Twitter Posts
  • Reddit Comments

Despite collecting less data than previous years, we still maintained a relatively stable sample size of 1.3%. This was due to the actual submitted vote count as reported by triple j also decreasing from 3,211,596 to 2,790,224.

Accuracy of Predicted Ranks

Making exact predictions is difficult, especially when you only have a small fraction of the data. The data will generally give you a good indication, but will never allow you to predict, with certainty, that a particular outcome will occur. This is the reason we implemented confidence intervals on our predictions for the top 10.
